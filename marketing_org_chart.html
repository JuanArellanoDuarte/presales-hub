Things You Probably Didn't Know About Us
TTEC Marketing Team by the Numbers

The Juan Situation

There are three Juans on this team: Caicedo in Portfolio Growth, Arellano in Demand Gen, and Escobar in Creative. If someone says "ask Juan," you're going to need a last name. We also have two Natalias, two Marias, and two Beths, but for some reason nobody ever gets confused about those.

The Bagtas Duo

Beth and Jayson Bagtas, both Creative Services Consultants, both in the Philippines, both on Nicolas's team. If you've ever gotten a creative asset from the Philippines, chances are a Bagtas touched it.

Colombia runs this department

31 of us sit in Colombia, making up 37% of the team. The US is close behind at 33%. Together, Bogotá and the States account for 70% of the entire org. The other 30% is spread across the Philippines, Egypt, the UK, Ireland, India, and Canada.

The Philippines is basically Creative's satellite office

All 11 of our Philippines-based teammates work in Creative. Nicolas Agudelo manages what is essentially a transatlantic studio spanning Bogotá, the US, and Manila.

Speaking of Creative

At 19 people (plus 2 open roles), it's the largest team by far. Nearly 1 in 4 people in Marketing sits in Creative. Nicolas, if you're reading this: yes, we know.

The title extremes

The longest title on the team, at 67 characters, belongs to Terra Gullet Brown: "Global Art Director, Internal Communications and Talent Acquisition." The shortest, at just 15 characters, is "Lead Specialist," shared by Ayman Ali and Donn Santiago, two people in completely different teams and countries who probably don't know they're title twins.

The word "Marketing" appears in 25 of our titles

Which makes sense, given that we are, in fact, the Marketing department. The runner-up is "Manager" at 18, followed by "Senior" at 17. "Designer" and "Graphic" are tied at 8 each, courtesy of Nicolas's empire.

We speak more than you think

English is the working language, but this team natively speaks at least Spanish, Tagalog, Arabic, and Hindi. If we ever need to launch a campaign in four languages at once, we wouldn't have to leave the building.

The weather gap is absurd

On any given day, someone on this team is at 38°C (100°F) in Cairo while someone else is at 14°C (57°F) in Dublin. Bogotá sits comfortably in the middle pretending the extremes don't exist. When someone says "nice weather today" on a team call, it means something completely different depending on who's talking.

We span 5 time zones on a normal workday

From Dublin and Cairo to Bogotá, Colorado, and Manila. Somewhere on this team, someone is always either starting their day or wrapping it up.
